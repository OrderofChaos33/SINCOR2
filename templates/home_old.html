NLP analysis of competitor reviews to find what customers actually complain about — and what they love. These are the gaps your marketing should exploit and the mistakes you should avoid.

Reputation Gaps

Where are competitors weak? Low review count, slow response times, unresolved complaints — these are opportunities. We flag the specific areas where you can own the narrative.

e.g. "3 of 5 competitors have not responded to negative reviews. Fast response = differentiation."

Market Gaps

Services customers are asking for that none of your competitors offer. Review mining often surfaces requests for services, hours, or options that are underserved in your area.

e.g. "Consistent requests for Sunday appointments across 4 competitors. None offer it."

For Skeptics
How We Actually
Get the Data

We understand if "AI-powered competitive intelligence" sounds like marketing speak. Here's what we actually do, technically, step by step.

💲 How We Get Pricing

We scrape competitor websites for service menus and pricing pages. We check Vagaro, Mindbody, and Square booking pages (often publicly accessible). We also analyze pricing mentioned in reviews ("paid $X for Y"). Where pricing is hidden, we flag it explicitly — we don't guess.

Website Scraping Booking Platform APIs Review Mining

🧠 How We Analyze Reviews

We use NLP (natural language processing) to categorize review text into themes: pricing, wait time, staff, cleanliness, results, parking, etc. We track sentiment and frequency across all competitors to find patterns — not just averages. A 4.2★ rating with 40% of reviews mentioning "rude staff" tells a very different story than a 4.2★ with no recurring themes.

NLP Classification Sentiment Analysis Theme Frequency

📣 How We Assess Marketing Activity

We check the Facebook Ads Library (public) for active ad campaigns. We look at Instagram post frequency and engagement rates. We check Google Business post activity, website blog recency, and SEO keyword presence. We estimate ad spend ranges from visible ad density and duration — not exact figures, but directional.

FB Ads Library Social Scraping SEO Tools

🔄 Data Freshness & Privacy

We pull fresh data for each report — no stale caches. Review data is typically 1–7 days old depending on source update cycles. We analyze business data only — no personal customer data, no individual tracking, no PII. We're analyzing how businesses present themselves publicly, not anything private.

FAQ
Real Questions.
Straight Answers.

Is this legal? Are you scraping private data? +
Yes, entirely legal. We only use publicly accessible data: Google Business listings, public review platforms, competitor websites, public social media posts, and the Facebook Ads Library (which Meta provides specifically for transparency). We don't access any private data, accounts, or information behind login walls. Competitive intelligence from public sources has been a normal business practice for decades — we just do it faster with software.
How accurate is the data? +
As accurate as the public sources allow. When data is clear and available (like a competitor posting their prices on their website), we report it exactly. When data is estimated (like ad spend), we give ranges and label them as estimates. When a competitor hides their pricing entirely, we say that too — hidden pricing is itself useful information. We'd rather be accurate about uncertainty than give you false confidence.
What if I don't like the report? +
30-day full refund. No questions, no retention calls, no forms. Email support@getsincor.com and we'll process it immediately. We're early-stage and building trust — we'd rather refund you and understand what was missing than have an unhappy customer. That said, if your market has genuinely limited public data (very small towns, very niche industries), we'll tell you upfront before you pay.
Can I see a sample report before buying? +
Yes. Email us at support@getsincor.com and tell us your industry, and we'll send you a sanitized sample report for a similar business type. No email opt-in gimmicks — just a real sample so you know what you're getting before you pay.
How often should I check my competitive landscape? +
For fast-moving markets (medical aesthetics, fitness, restaurants, real estate), monthly is ideal — competitors adjust pricing seasonally, run promotions, and respond to market shifts. For more stable industries (law, accounting, trade services), quarterly is usually sufficient. A one-time report is great if you're just starting, entering a new market, or making a major pricing decision. The monthly subscription gives you the trend data, which is often more valuable than a single snapshot.
How do you identify my 5 "closest" competitors? +
We use geographic proximity (within your city/area), service overlap (same primary services), and customer crossover signals (review text that mentions nearby alternatives). For the one-time report, we pick the 5 most relevant competitors based on these signals. On the Monthly plan, you can also request specific competitors to include in your analysis.
What industries do you support? +
Any local service business where pricing and competitive position matter. We work best with: medical aesthetics / med spas, gyms and fitness studios, real estate agents and brokerages, dental and chiropractic, salons and spas, restaurants and cafes, law firms, and home services. If you're unsure whether your industry is a good fit, email us before buying — we'll be honest.
Can agencies or consultants resell SINCOR reports? +
Yes. We have a white-label partner program where agencies can order reports under their own brand and deliver them to clients. If you're a marketing consultant, business coach, or agency serving local businesses, this could be a valuable addition to your offering. Email partners@getsincor.com and we'll share our current partner pricing and terms.
